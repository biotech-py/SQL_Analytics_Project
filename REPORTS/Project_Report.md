# SQL Analytics Project

## Project Overview

This project analyzes a retail sales dataset using SQL and SQLite to extract meaningful business insights. The objective is to evaluate revenue generation, profitability, customer behavior, product performance, and regional sales trends through structured SQL queries.

The analysis demonstrates the practical use of SQL for business intelligence and decision-making by transforming raw transactional data into actionable insights.

---

# 1. Business Overview

## Objective

The primary objectives of this project are:

* Analyze overall business performance.
* Evaluate revenue and profitability.
* Identify top-performing products and customers.
* Compare regional performance.
* Understand the impact of discounts on profit.
* Apply advanced SQL concepts for business analysis.

---

# 2. Dataset Overview

The dataset contains retail sales transactions with information related to:

* Orders
* Customers
* Products
* Categories
* Regions
* Discounts
* Sales
* Profit

### Key Metrics

| Metric        | Value         |
| ------------- | ------------- |
| Total Records | 9,994         |
| Total Revenue | $2,297,200.86 |
| Total Profit  | $286,397.02   |
| Unique Orders | 5,009         |

---

# 3. Revenue Analysis

## Purpose

Evaluate the overall revenue generated by the business.

### Findings

* Total revenue generated was **$2.29 million**.
* The company maintained a large transaction volume across multiple regions.
* Average sales per transaction were approximately **$229.86**.

### Business Insight

The organization demonstrates strong sales performance with a diversified customer base and consistent transaction activity.

---

# 4. Category Performance Analysis

## Revenue by Category

Revenue was analyzed across product categories.

### Findings

| Category        | Revenue  |
| --------------- | -------- |
| Technology      | Highest  |
| Furniture       | Moderate |
| Office Supplies | Lowest   |

### Business Insight

Technology products are the primary revenue drivers and contribute significantly to overall business growth.

---

## Profit by Category

Profitability was evaluated for each category.

### Findings

| Category        | Profitability |
| --------------- | ------------- |
| Technology      | Highest       |
| Office Supplies | High          |
| Furniture       | Lowest        |

### Business Insight

Although Furniture generates substantial revenue, it produces relatively low profit compared to other categories.

---

## Profit Margin Analysis

Profit margin was calculated as:

Profit Margin (%) = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

### Findings

| Category        | Profit Margin (%) |
| --------------- | ----------------- |
| Technology      | 17.40             |
| Office Supplies | 17.04             |
| Furniture       | 2.49              |

### Business Insight

Technology and Office Supplies maintain healthy margins, whereas Furniture may require pricing or cost optimization.

---

# 5. Regional Sales Analysis

### Findings

## Profit by Region

Profitability was analyzed region-wise.

# 7. Customer Analysis

## Top Revenue-Generating Customers

Customer purchases were aggregated to identify high-value clients.

### Findings

Several customers contribute substantially more revenue than the average customer.

### Business Insight

Retaining high-value customers should be a priority because they generate a disproportionately large share of revenue.

---

# 8. Customer Segmentation

## Objective

Customers were categorized according to spending levels.

### Segmentation Logic

| Revenue Range   | Segment      |
| --------------- | ------------ |
| ≥ $10,000       | High Value   |
| $5,000 - $9,999 | Medium Value |
| < $5,000        | Low Value    |

### Findings

* A small number of customers belong to the High Value segment.
* Most customers fall within Medium and Low Value categories.

### Business Insight

Targeted marketing campaigns can improve retention and increase spending among medium-value customers.

---

# 9. Product Performance Analysis

## Top Products by Revenue

Products were ranked based on total revenue generated.

### Findings

Several premium products generated significantly higher revenue than the average product.

### Business Insight

These products represent critical contributors to overall business success and should receive priority in inventory and promotional planning.

---

## Top Products by Profit

Products were also ranked according to profitability.

### Findings

High-revenue products are not always the most profitable.

### Business Insight

Revenue should not be used as the sole performance metric. Profitability provides a more accurate measure of business value.

---

# 10. Loss-Making Products

## Objective

Identify products generating negative profit.

### Findings

Multiple products produced losses despite generating sales.

### Business Insight

Potential reasons include:

* Excessive discounts
* High operational costs
* Inefficient pricing strategies

These products require detailed review.

---

# 11. Discount Analysis

## Objective

Evaluate the relationship between discounts and profitability.

### Findings

Products receiving higher discounts often generated lower profits.

### Business Insight

Aggressive discounting may increase sales volume but can negatively impact profitability.

Businesses should balance revenue growth with margin protection.

---

# 12. Average Order Value Analysis

### Findings

Average order value varied across regions.
